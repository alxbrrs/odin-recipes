Nachos

Description

Nachos are great. I love nachos. One of the greatest things about nachos is that although there probably is a wrong way to make them, if you decide you don't care what other people think you can do whatever you think.

For example, I once had a flatmate from france who really liked cooking and tried to tell me that chucking some cheese on top of a plate of tortilla chips and throwing it in the microwave didn't count as cooking. He can piss off, I was hungover and it was declicious.

Ingredients

  • Tortilla chips - as many as you want depending on how hungry you are/if you're being forced to share. Just use the whole bag, I won't tell.
  • Cheese (lots), grated- I use cheddar, but I guess you can do what you want here, as long as it's something that will melt. Gouda maybe? See what you've got lurking in your fridge.
  • Optional other stuff and toppings
    • Spring onions ("scallions")/red onion, sliced or diced, whatever you want really (I like onions, ok?)
    • Jalapeno, sliced. I know the n should have a tilde over it, but my German keyboard doesn't want to do that right now.
    • Coriander ("cilantro"), because it's delicious
    • Guacamole
    • Salsa, or pico de gallo
    • Sour cream
    • Chili con carne, but you'll have to find a recipe for that somewhere else
    • Anything else you can think of or find that sounds good. I don't know what's in your fridge.

Steps

There's more than one way to do this, depending on how fancy you're feeling.

The super lazy way

  1. Put tortilla chips into a microwave safe bowl.
  2. Add cheese - mix some in so you don't end up disappointed when you've eaten the top layer and then you have to eat the rest all dry and cheeseless.
  3. Put it in the microwave. Honestly, I have no idea how long or what setting - try like, 30 seconds on full power and see what it looks like from there.

The slightly fancier way

  1. Preheat the oven to, idk, 180 degrees? Celsius, you Americans are on your own there.
  2. Put the nachos into an oven proof dish and add the cheese - again, maybe mix some in so there's no sad cheeseless bits underneath?
  3. Add any toppings you think should be hot. Personally I'd add them all after it has been in the oven, though.
  4. Sling it in the oven for a bit. Again, I don't know how long, just keep an eye on it and take it out when the cheese looks all melty and delicious.
  5. Remove from oven, add toppings, and eat before anyone notices you have nachos and asks you to share.
